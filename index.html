Bridging the Gap: From Classroom to Newsroom

Empowering the next generation of Nigerian media leaders — led by women.

In Nigeria’s evolving media landscape, the story is often told — but the storytellers remain unbalanced. Young Female Journalists Network (YFJN) exists to correct gender disparity by transforming ambitious students into high‑impact professionals. We don’t just bridge the gap between the classroom and the newsroom; we build the bridge.

Our mission

To cultivate a new vanguard of female journalists equipped with the strategic foresight, ethical grounding, and technical mastery required to lead national discourse and contribute to sustainable nation‑building.

Navigating the Industry

Psychological and professional blueprints to identify emerging opportunities and navigate challenges with resilience.

Ethos‑Driven Mentorship

Mentorship by seasoned veterans — grounded in integrity, civic responsibility, and newsroom standards.

Practical Excellence

Hands‑on experience and real‑world assignments that make members day‑one ready for modern media.

Gender Parity Advocacy

Increasing the visibility and capability of female reporters — restructuring newsroom representation from the ground up.
