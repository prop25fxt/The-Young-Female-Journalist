Executive leadership

Guiding the vision, integrity, and future of the Young Female Journalists Network.

Hadiza Onido

Convener & Executive Director

Journalist | Communications Consultant | Specialist in Peace & Conflict Resolution

Hadiza Onido is a journalist and strategic communications expert whose work sits at the intersection of media, gender equity, peacebuilding, and national development. She serves as Convener of YFJN, bridging professional experience with the next generation of female media leaders.

Professional impact

  • Women Page Columnist — NUJ The Defender Magazine
  • Contributor: The Nation Newspaper (Guest Columnist)
  • Contributor: Blueprint Newspaper (Guest Columnist)
  • 21st Century Chronicle (Staff Reporter / Author)
  • Communications Consultant — British Council (BAY states reintegration initiative)

Academic background & environmental advocacy

Hadiza holds postgraduate degrees in Conflict, Peace, and Strategy; International Affairs; and Leadership and Strategic Studies. She is a Fellow of the Nigerian Defence Academy (NDA), researching the intersection between soft power and national prosperity. In 2025, she founded a specialized shelter for endangered plants to support conservation action.

“We are not just training reporters; we are preparing the female voices that will shape the narrative of our nation.”

— Hadiza Onido

Binta Shama

National Publicity Secretary

Media Strategist | Investigative Journalist

Binta Shama serves as the institutional voice of the Network. With over nine years of experience, she is known for human‑angle storytelling that transforms complex data into compelling narratives for the public and policy‑makers.

Portfolio of impact

  • Investigative Reporting • Feature Writing • Broadcast Media
  • Science, Technology & Innovation (STI)
  • Governance & Human Rights
  • Conflict & Humanitarian Reporting

Driving innovation & inclusion

In her executive role, Binta leads external communications and mentors young female journalists to develop technical skills and strategic newsroom thinking — grounded in the belief that diversity of storytellers strengthens society.

“Journalism is the bridge between silence and justice. At YFJN, my mission is to ensure the female perspective leads the narrative.”

— Binta Shama
